## Slide 4: How It Works — Two Platforms, One Mission

### "I Want Help" (Provider Side)
- Organizations, verified NGOs, individuals in need
- **Create tasks**: "50 families need food baskets in Zarqa"
- Set how many volunteers are needed
- Verify deliveries when completed

### "I Want to Help" (Helper Side)
- Individual volunteers, donors, community members
- **Get notified** when tasks are posted
- **Accept a task** — deliver food, donate clothes, volunteer time
- **Submit proof** — photo, location
- **See your impact** — every contribution tracked on blockchain

```
Organization creates task
        ↓
Volunteers get notified
        ↓
20 drivers accept → each takes a portion
        ↓
Each volunteer delivers + submits proof
        ↓
Organization confirms each delivery
        ↓
Blockchain records EVERYTHING
        ↓
Every volunteer sees their impact
```

## Slide 5: The Blockchain — Proof, Not Payments

> "We don't use blockchain for payments. We use it for something more valuable — **proof**. Proof that the work happened. Proof that can't be edited, deleted, or faked."

**What blockchain tracks:**

| Step | What's Recorded | Permanent? |
|------|----------------|------------|
| Task Created | "50 food baskets needed in Zarqa" | Forever |
| Volunteer Accepted | "Ahmad accepted — delivering 3 baskets" | Forever |
| Donation Recorded | "10 JDs donated via Jordan Humanitarian Aid" | Forever |
| Proof Submitted | Photo hash of delivered baskets | Forever |
| Delivery Confirmed | Organization verified receipt | Forever |

**What blockchain does NOT do:**
- No payments through crypto
- No tokens or wallets for users
- No cryptocurrency involved
- Just **pure, immutable proof of impact**

### The Impact Answer

The donor asks: *"Where did my 10 JDs go?"*

**Takafol answers:**

```
  → Delivered by: Volunteer Ahmad
  → Confirmed: March 3, 2026
  → Blockchain Proof: 0x7a3f... [View on Chain]
```

No other platform gives you this answer.
